Welcome Message

The website Thriving Together is for professional early childhood education and it intends to support teachers in their understanding of the family and community contexts which are diverse and have a great impact on the children's lives in the current Australian society. The resource is a combination of research findings, sociological theory, policy analysis, and practical strategies that will enable educators to work with children and families in a confident, respectful, and collaborative manner.

About This Resource

Purpose of This Website

This website aims to deepen educators‚Äô understanding of the contexts that influence children‚Äôs development and wellbeing, provide clear explanations of complex social issues, introduce evidence-based strategies for inclusive practice, strengthen partnerships with families and communities, and share high-quality resources for children aged birth to five.

Who This Website Is For

This resource supports early childhood educators, pre-service teachers, educational leaders, centre directors, allied health and family support professionals, and students enrolled in EPRO204 or similar units. The tone is formal and suitable for both professional and academic contexts.

How to Navigate This Website

The website is organised into five major family and community contexts: Economic, Social, Cultural & Diversity, Health & Wellbeing, and Crisis & Emergency. Each section includes an overview, impacts on children and families, relevant Australian policies, five educator strategies, five organisations, twelve children‚Äôs resources, and links to EYLF V2.0 and Practice Principles.

Our Vision

Every child and family should be welcomed, valued, understood, and supported. Strong, respectful community connections enable educators to help children participate fully and joyfully in their lives.

Professional Commitment

This website upholds Australian early childhood professional standards, reflects ethical and strength-based practice, promotes diversity, inclusion, reconciliation, and equity, and adheres to APA 7 academic conventions.
